Meatless Burger Patty

Jul 8, 2021 3 min read Others

Made your soy milk at home and have no idea how to settle the pulp? Let's transform it into some nice dishes!

What is Okara?

Okara („Åä„Åã„Çâ) is made of soybean pulp, which is the remaining of the soy milk after being blended into soybeans. As the byproduct of soybeans, okara is rich in nutrients, especially protein, thus people like to use it to make keto desserts. For more health benefits can refer here.

How to store Okara?

After I have filtered my soy milk, I will quickly store them into separate plastic containers and freeze them so that I don't need to thaw the whole thing and take the amount required for the batch of a dish.

It is recommended to store the fresh okara in the freezer immediately to lengthen the shelf span.

Time Required

  • Preparation time - 15 minutes
  • Cooking time - 10 minutes

Ingredients

The measurements below are based on 4 servings (which means 4 pieces of burger patty).

You will need:

  • 120g Okara (fresh/store-bought)
  • 20g Black beans (soaked, cooked)
  • 1 Carrot (shredded, diced)
  • 4 Shiitake mushrooms (soaked)
  • 20g General purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp Rice flour

Optional:

  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 6 cloves of garlic (diced)

Adjust according to your taste preferences:

  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Black / white pepper powder
  • other spices

Cooking Instructions

  1. Slightly heat the fresh okara to vaporize the water inside and ensure it is cooked.
  2. Saut√© the garlic and onion until fragrant. Then add the carrot and shiitake mushrooms into the cooking pan.
  3. Pulse a quarter of your black beans with a blender. ¬†You may choose not to blend all the black beans so that your patty looks darker (which resembles the color of a real meat patty)
  4. Mix the okara, vegetables, and black beans in a bigger pot. Add general-purpose flour and rice flour accordingly.
  5. Add general-purpose flour to make the ingredients clump. Add rice flour if you prefer crunchiness. Stir the mixture until it is well combined.
  6. Shape your mixture into a patty-like shape.
  7. In a non-stick pan, add sunflower oil until it slightly covers the bottom of the pan. When the oil is heated, put your patty into the pan. Flip the patty when the heating side is no longer sticky.
  8. Serve them up after they are cooked thoroughly.

Serving suggestions

You may serve the patty in two ways, either:

  • Bigger version - as a burger patty
  • Mini version - as nuggets

either way works!
